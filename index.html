Habilis-β: A Fast-Motion and Long-Lasting On-Device Vision-Language-Action Model

Tommoro Robotics1,
Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

We introduce Habilis-β, a fast-motion, long-lasting, and on-device vision-language-action (VLA) model designed for real-world deployment. Current VLA evaluation remains largely confined to single-trial success rates under curated resets, which fails to capture the fast-motion and long-lasting capabilities essential for practical operation. To address this, we introduce the Productivity–Reliability Plane (PRP) evaluating performance through Tasks per Hour (TPH) and Mean Time Between Intervention (MTBI) under a continuous-run protocol that demands both high-speed execution and sustained robustness. Habilis-β achieves high-performance by integrating language-free pre-training on large-scale play data for robust interaction priors with post-training on cyclic demonstrations that capture state drift across consecutive task iterations. The system further employs ESPADA for phase-adaptive motion shaping to accelerate free-space transit, utilizes rectified-flow distillation to enable high-frequency control on edge devices, and incorporates classifier-free guidance (CFG) as a deployment-time knob to dynamically balance instruction adherence and learned interaction priors. In 1-hour continuous-run evaluations, Habilis-β significantly outperforms π0.5 in both simulation and real-world environments. In simulation, Habilis-β improves MTBI to 39.2s and TPH to 572.6 (from 30.5s and 120.5 for π0.5), while on a real-world humanoid logistics workflow, it achieves 137.4s MTBI and 124 TPH, surpassing π0.5's 46.1s MTBI and 19 TPH. Finally, Habilis-β achieves the highest reported performance on the standard RoboTwin 2.0 leaderboard across representative tasks, validating its effectiveness in complex manipulation scenarios.

Habilis-β

Robots do not fail because they cannot complete a single task once. They fail because they cannot do it repeatedly, quickly, and reliably in the real world.

Habilis-β is built around this observation. Instead of optimizing for isolated success rates under curated resets, we design and evaluate a system for sustained autonomous operation. The goal is not just to finish a task — but to keep running.

Rethinking Evaluation

Most robot learning benchmarks measure single-trial success. But real deployment is continuous:

  • Tasks repeat.
  • Initial states drift.
  • Small errors accumulate.
  • Recovery becomes necessary.

We introduce a continuous-run protocol, where a robot operates for a fixed wall-clock duration without manual resets. Performance is measured along two axes:

  • Productivity — Tasks per Hour (TPH)
  • Reliability — Mean Time Between Intervention (MTBI)

Together, they form the Productivity–Reliability Plane (PRP). Deployment readiness means moving up and to the right.

Habilis-β Structure

A Three-Stage Training Strategy

Habilis-β integrates multiple components into a unified system:

1. Play Pretraining

We first learn from large-scale, language-free play data. Play data is unstructured teleoperation without explicit task segmentation. It provides a broad interaction prior — covering regrasping, repositioning, and local corrections.

2. Cyclic Task Alignment

We then train on cyclic demonstrations: continuous trajectories where the same task is executed repeatedly without resetting. This exposes the model to state drift and recovery behavior across cycles.

3. Efficient Action Generation

Finally, we compress the action model using rectified-flow distillation, enabling low-latency inference and high-frequency control suitable for real-world deployment.

A Deployment-Oriented Architecture

Habilis-β follows a prefix–suffix design:

  • A pre-trained vision-language model encodes multimodal inputs.
  • A lightweight action expert generates short action chunks.
  • High-frequency re-inference maintains responsiveness.
  • Optional classifier-free guidance adjusts instruction strength at runtime.

The entire perception-to-action loop runs locally, ensuring predictable latency and stable control.

From Benchmark Success to Sustained Operation

Under continuous-run evaluation, Habilis-β demonstrates substantial improvements in both throughput and intervention-free runtime compared to strong VLA baselines — in simulation and on real humanoid hardware.

The key shift is conceptual:

We do not treat tasks as independent episodes. We treat deployment as a continuous process. Habilis-β is designed accordingly.
